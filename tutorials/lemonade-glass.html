How to draw... a glass of lemonade

Build a summer drink from ellipses and light transparent layers, then add the straw, lemon, ice, and just enough yellow pencil to make it refreshing.

25 minEasy-medium
Start drawing

From the archive

Let's draw lemonade

Treat this as one small practice round: build the subject lightly, notice the shapes, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional yellow colored pencilCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the glass shape

    Draw a wide rim ellipse, two slightly tapered side lines, and a smaller base ellipse. Add a light center guide through the glass.

    Sketch tip: Make the top ellipse wider than the bottom one. That taper is what makes the glass feel tall.

  2. 02

    Set the lemonade level

    Add a curved liquid line across the glass, then reinforce the lower rim and base so the glass still feels transparent.

    Sketch tip: Let the liquid curve echo the rim ellipse. Matching those curves keeps the lemonade from looking flat.

  3. 03

    Add the straw

    Draw a diagonal straw from the bottom of the glass up through the rim, then bend the top section outward.

    Sketch tip: Keep both straw edges parallel. A narrow tube reads better than a single heavy line.

  4. 04

    Draw lemon and ice

    Clip a lemon wheel onto the rim, divide it into wedge segments, and sketch a few tilted ice cubes inside the drink.

    Sketch tip: Let the ice overlap the straw and liquid line lightly. Those overlaps sell the clear-glass effect.

  5. 05

    Add droplets and color

    Scatter tiny condensation dots on the glass, shade a soft table shadow, and add light yellow pencil to the lemonade and lemon slice.

    Sketch tip: Leave white gaps around the ice and rim. Transparent drawings need untouched paper as much as color.

  6. 06

    Finish the lemonade sketch

    Darken the keeper contours, clarify the straw and lemon overlaps, and strengthen only the yellow shading and soft table shadow already in place.

    Sketch tip: Do not outline every droplet equally. A few dark marks plus many pale ones feel more natural.
