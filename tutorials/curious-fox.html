How to draw... a curious fox

Build this woodland friend from a few loose shapes, then bring it to life with warm, scribbly color.

25 minEasy
Start drawing

From the archive

Let's draw a fox

Treat this as one small practice round: build the subject lightly, notice the shapes, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Orange, cream, and dark brown colored pencilsCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the head and body

    Draw a head circle, then tuck a tilted egg shape behind it for the seated body. Let the shapes overlap.

    Sketch tip: Keep it light: These are measuring lines. Draw through the shapes instead of trying to make them perfect.

  2. 02

    Build the fox face

    Add two tall ear triangles. From the lower-left side of the circle, pull out a long wedge for the muzzle.

    Sketch tip: Check the angle: The nose lands slightly below the center of the head circle.

  3. 03

    Connect the body

    Curve the chest down from the jaw, add two straight front legs, then use the egg shape to guide the rounded back.

    Sketch tip: Find the floor: End both front legs at the same height before adding the paws.

  4. 04

    Wrap the tail forward

    Start at the upper back, swing the line around the haunch, and bring the fluffy tail across both paws.

    Sketch tip: Draw both edges: The second curve gives the tail its weight. Keep the tip broad rather than pointy.

  5. 05

    Choose the lines to keep

    Darken the useful contours, then add two simple eyes, the nose, and the white chest shape. Leave some pale guides behind.

    Sketch tip: Don't trace everything: A broken, doubled line feels more like a sketch than one perfect outline.

  6. 06

    Scribble in a little color

    Loosely hatch orange over the ears, back, and tail. Leave the face, chest, and tail tip mostly paper-white.

    Sketch tip: Stop early: White gaps and visible graphite are part of the finished sketch, not mistakes to cover.
