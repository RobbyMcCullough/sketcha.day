Day 007 Wednesday, June 17

How to draw
a quiet
farm lane

Use a simple horizon, lane edges, and blocky building shapes to turn a blank page into a small countryside scene.

35 minMedium-hard8 steps
Start drawing

From the archive

Let's draw a quiet farm lane

Work lightly through the construction, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional red, green, and brown pencilsCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the horizon and lane

    Draw a low rolling horizon, then pull two lane edges from the barn area down toward the bottom of the page.

    Sketch tip: Let the lane get wider as it comes forward. That widening is the simplest way to show depth.

  2. 02

    Block the barn and silo

    Add a tall silo rectangle on the left and a wide barn block on the right, keeping both sitting on the same ground line.

    Sketch tip: Use boxy shapes first. The scene will feel calmer if the big pieces are placed before the details.

  3. 03

    Build the roof and doors

    Turn the barn block into a gable roof, round the top of the silo, and add the big barn door and small upper window.

    Sketch tip: Keep the roof peak above the door center. That vertical alignment keeps the barn from leaning.

  4. 04

    Add the fence posts

    March fence posts from the foreground toward the barn, making each post smaller as it gets farther away.

    Sketch tip: The rails should angle toward the lane's vanishing area, not run straight across the page.

  5. 05

    Texture the dirt lane

    Add short broken strokes across the road and a few pebbly marks that get smaller near the barn.

    Sketch tip: Use fewer marks in the distance. Tiny repeated texture can flatten the perspective.

  6. 06

    Add shrubs and field edges

    Sketch low shrubs around the barn, soft grass along the lane, and curved field lines that follow the hills.

    Sketch tip: Keep the background lines lighter than the fence and barn so the scene keeps its depth.

  7. 07

    Shade the buildings

    Add vertical siding on the barn, light grid marks on the silo, and darker strokes under the roof edges.

    Sketch tip: Save your darkest lines for the barn door, roof edge, and foreground fence posts.

  8. 08

    Finish with soft color

    Add loose red pencil to the barn, green to the grass and hills, and warm brown strokes through the dirt lane.

    Sketch tip: Leave plenty of paper showing. A quiet scene feels better with restrained color than full coverage.
