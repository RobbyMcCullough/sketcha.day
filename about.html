The people behind the pencils

Daily drawing practice, made by a family that cares about learning.

Sketcha.day is made by Robby McCullough, a lifelong doodler, designer, and web guy who believes a drawing habit gets stronger through small, repeatable practice.

Tracie is a mom and early childhood educator. Her perspective helps keep the lessons clear, encouraging, and useful for artists who are still building confidence, including kids and grown-ups drawing beside them.

Together, we want Sketcha.day to feel like a friendly sketchbook ritual: simple shapes, practical steps, honest materials, and finished drawings that still leave room for your own hand.

Why these lessons are built this way

Lifelong doodlingRobby brings years of sketchbook habit, visual problem-solving, and a bias toward approachable creative practice.
Design and web craftThe pages are structured to be clear, scannable, accessible, and useful without needing a video or app.
Early-childhood lensTracie's background helps us value clear steps, patient pacing, and encouragement over perfection.
Real-life rhythmThe lessons are meant to fit into ordinary days, usually with a pencil, paper, and a few quiet minutes.
