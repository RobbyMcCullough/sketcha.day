How to draw... a porch swing with a cushion

Begin with one light box and a set of chain guides, then turn that structure into a hanging wooden bench before adding the arms, cushion, slats, and a quiet wash of pencil color. The three-quarter angle makes this a useful practice sketch for keeping long edges and repeated gaps consistent.

Pencil ready?

Let's draw a porch swing with a cushion

Treat this as one small practice round: build the subject lightly, notice the shapes, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional pale teal and brown pencilsCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Set the swing guides

    Draw a light three-quarter box for the seat, raise a tall back guide, and drop two faint pairs of chain lines from above.

    Sketch tip: Ghost the seat's long front edge before committing, then compare the empty wedge at each side. Keeping those negative spaces similar will make the bench feel balanced.

  2. 02

    Shape the hanging bench

    Turn the box and back guides into a simple wooden bench silhouette, keeping the established chain lines attached near all four corners.

    Sketch tip: Use one relaxed pull for each long edge and let the back lean only slightly. The seat and top rail should share the same three-quarter direction.

  3. 03

    Add the armrests

    Build one armrest on each side and connect each to the existing seat with a short front support.

    Sketch tip: Mark both armrest heights before drawing their edges. Comparing the space beneath each arm is quicker than measuring the supports.

  4. 04

    Lay in cushion and slats

    Place a soft rectangular cushion on the seat and divide the established back into clear wooden slats.

    Sketch tip: Set the first and last slat, then fit the others between them. Let the cushion corners round gently so it reads as fabric against the straighter timber.

  5. 05

    Shade the porch swing

    Add pale teal pencil to the existing cushion, a restrained brown tint to the wood, and a soft graphite shadow under the swing.

    Sketch tip: Pull color strokes along each form: lengthwise on the boards and across the cushion's soft plane. Keep some paper showing so the sketch stays light.

  6. 06

    Settle the swing sketch

    Strengthen the keeper contours and clarify the established chains, armrests, slats, cushion color, wood tone, and shadow.

    Sketch tip: Stop before adding pillows, plants, railings, or porch posts. The chains, slatted bench, and one comfortable cushion already tell the whole story.
