How to draw... sunglasses on a towel

Use two simple lens circles and one tilted towel rectangle to build a sunny sketch that still feels light and handmade.

25 minEasy-medium
Start drawing

Pencil ready?

Let's draw sunglasses on a towel

Work lightly through the construction, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional blending stump or tissueCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the towel and lenses

    Draw a tilted rectangle for the towel, then place two light rounded lens guides across the middle.

    Sketch tip: Let the towel angle first. The sunglasses will feel more relaxed if they follow that slant.

  2. 02

    Connect the sunglass frame

    Wrap a rim around each lens and connect them with a short bridge between the circles.

    Sketch tip: Keep both rims the same thickness. A second light pass around each lens is enough.

  3. 03

    Fold back the arms

    Add the folded side arms peeking from behind the lenses, following the towel's angle.

    Sketch tip: Tuck the arms under the frame edges so they look folded back, not sticking straight out.

  4. 04

    Add towel details

    Refine the towel edges, sketch a few long stripes, and add little fringe marks along the ends.

    Sketch tip: Do not make every fringe identical. Small differences keep the cloth feeling hand drawn.

  5. 05

    Shade the lenses

    Fill both lenses with soft dark graphite and leave a small pale highlight near the top of each lens.

    Sketch tip: Shade around the highlights instead of drawing white shapes on top. Untouched paper stays brightest.

  6. 06

    Finish the sunny sketch

    Darken the keeper contours, strengthen the towel texture, and add restrained shadows under the frame and fringe.

    Sketch tip: Stop before the towel gets too busy. The dark lenses should remain the strongest value.
