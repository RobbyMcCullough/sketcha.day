How to draw... an apple turnover on a plate

Start with the plate and folded pastry as simple shapes, then press in the edge, add the filling slit and fork, and finish with crumbs and warm color. Your pastry can be a little uneven; the crimped edge and plate are what make it readable.

25 minEasy-medium

Pencil ready?

Let's draw an apple turnover on a plate

Treat this as one small practice round: build the subject lightly, notice the shapes, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional golden-brown colored pencilCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the plate and pastry

    Draw a shallow oval for the plate, then place a light triangular turnover body inside it.

    Sketch tip: Keep the triangle low and wide. A flatter pastry feels more believable on the plate.

  2. 02

    Fold the pastry form

    Round the pastry corners and draw the folded seam over the construction lines.

    Sketch tip: Let the seam follow the outside edge. That makes the turnover look folded instead of like a plain triangle.

  3. 03

    Press the edge

    Add small crimp marks along the folded edge, then clean up the plate rim around the pastry.

    Sketch tip: Use short repeated marks for the crimping. They do not need to be perfectly even.

  4. 04

    Add filling and fork

    Cut a short slit near the top of the turnover, show a peek of apple filling, and angle a simple fork beside the plate.

    Sketch tip: Make the fork narrow and simple. Four readable tines are enough for this sketch.

  5. 05

    Scatter crumbs and shade

    Add a few crumbs on the plate, then shade the pastry with light golden pencil and soft graphite under the rim.

    Sketch tip: Leave pale paper showing through the color. Broken shading keeps the pastry sketchy and warm.

  6. 06

    Finish the apple turnover sketch

    Darken the keeper contours, sharpen the crimp marks, warm the existing pastry shading, and clarify the fork and crumbs.

    Sketch tip: Stop before adding a table setting or extra fruit. The plate, fork, crimping, and filling slit already carry the subject.
