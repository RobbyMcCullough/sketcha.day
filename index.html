Day 001 Monday, June 15

How to draw
a curious fox

Build this woodland friend from a few loose shapes, then bring it to life with warm, scribbly color.

25 min Easy going 6 steps
Start sketching

There are no bad lines here.

Draw lightly · Stay curious · Make it yours

Pencil ready?

Let's draw a fox

Use light pressure for the first few steps. Those early lines are scaffolding, not commitments.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilHB or 2B is perfect
  • Drawing paperAny sketchbook will do
  • EraserKneaded or regular
  • Colored pencilsOrange, cream & dark brown

No colored pencils? Keep it graphite. Value and texture can do all the work.

  1. 01

    Start with two shapes

    Draw a circle for the head and a larger, tilted oval for the body. Let them overlap a little.

    Artist tip: Use your whole arm to draw circles. Loose shapes have more life.

  2. 02

    Add the muzzle and ears

    Pull a soft wedge out from the circle for the snout. Add two tall triangles, leaning them slightly apart.

    Notice: Fox ears are delightfully oversized. Don't be shy.

  3. 03

    Place the legs

    Drop two narrow front legs from the chest. A big curved line behind them becomes the folded back leg.

    Keep checking: The paws should land on the same imaginary floor.

  4. 04

    Sweep in the tail

    Make one generous S-curve from the fox's back, around the body, and across the front paws.

    Go big: The tail is the foxiest part. Exaggerate its volume.

  5. 05

    Refine the face & fur

    Add almond-shaped eyes, a dark nose, and the cream chest patch. Break clean outlines with a few furry flicks.

    Less is more: A handful of fur marks will suggest thousands.

  6. 06

    Add color & texture

    Build orange in light layers. Leave the muzzle, chest, and tail tip cream, then deepen the legs and ears with charcoal.

    Final touch: Let some graphite show through. It keeps the drawing lively.
