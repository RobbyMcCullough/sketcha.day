Let's draw a cozy teacup

Work lightly through the construction, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional coral and blue pencilsCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place two ovals

    Draw a small oval for the cup opening and a wider oval below it for the saucer. Add a light center line through both.

    Sketch tip: Keep the two ovals centered on the same guide line so the cup does not lean.

  2. 02

    Shape the cup bowl

    Use the top oval as the rim, then pull two gently slanted sides down to a small curved base.

    Sketch tip: Make both sides lean inward by the same amount before darkening them.

  3. 03

    Add the handle

    Draw a large C-shaped handle on the right side, then echo it inside with a smaller curve.

    Sketch tip: Attach the handle near the rim and lower cup wall so it feels connected, not floating.

  4. 04

    Build the saucer and tea

    Darken the saucer oval, add a smaller inner oval, and draw a light tea surface inside the cup rim.

    Sketch tip: Keep these ovals flatter than circles. Flat ovals make the cup feel level.

  5. 05

    Add steam and a heart

    Sketch three soft steam wisps above the rim, then add a tiny heart centered on the cup.

    Sketch tip: Steam lines should stay lighter than the cup outline. They are atmosphere, not structure.

  6. 06

    Shade the cup and saucer

    Darken the cup sides, saucer edge, and handle, then add loose graphite shading under the cup and across the saucer.

    Sketch tip: Stop shading before the paper gets gray everywhere. The white gaps keep it sketchy.

  7. 07

    Finish the cozy details

    Add restrained color to the cup, saucer, and tea surface, then choose the keeper lines around the rim, heart, and handle.

    Sketch tip: Use color as short strokes over the graphite rather than filling the whole cup solid.
