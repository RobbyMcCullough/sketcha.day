How to draw
a detailed
pinecone

Build the pinecone from one tilted egg shape, then connect simple scale blocks into layered flaps with shadow and texture.

30 minMedium8 steps

Let's draw a detailed pinecone

Work lightly through the construction, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional brown pencilCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the tilted egg

    Draw one light egg shape leaning slightly right, then add a curved center line and a low guide line for the bottom row of scales.

    Sketch tip: Keep the guide shape wider near the bottom. A pinecone feels sturdier when the base has more weight.

  2. 02

    Block the lower scales

    Start at the bottom edge and draw three to five chunky scale blocks, each shaped like a small folded shield.

    Sketch tip: Let the blocks overlap instead of lining them up evenly. The staggered edges make the cone look natural.

  3. 03

    Stack the middle scales

    Add two staggered rows through the center, placing each new scale between two lower scales so the pattern climbs upward.

    Sketch tip: Leave pale diamond-shaped tops on the scales. Those light planes help the pinecone read before you add shading.

  4. 04

    Place the small edge scales

    Work outward with smaller scale faces near the top, left edge, and right edge so the guide shape starts to disappear.

    Sketch tip: Use fewer details at the far edges. Too many tiny scales can make the silhouette noisy.

  5. 05

    Connect the scale flaps

    Pull short back edges from each scale face and link neighboring rows with shallow underside curves.

    Sketch tip: Think of each scale as a little flap, not a flat tile. The back edge is what makes it overlap.

  6. 06

    Darken the pockets

    Shade the tucked spaces between rows, especially under the front edges of the larger middle scales.

    Sketch tip: Push the darkest graphite into the spaces behind the scales. That contrast gives the drawing its depth.

  7. 07

    Add grain and cast shadow

    Use short directional pencil strokes across the scales, then sketch a loose shadow underneath the pinecone.

    Sketch tip: Keep your texture strokes angled with the scale, not straight across the page.

  8. 08

    Finish with brown pencil

    Add brown pencil to a few front planes, deepen selected outlines, and leave the highlighted scale tips mostly paper-white.

    Sketch tip: Stop before every scale is colored. Uneven color keeps the sketch quick and handmade.
