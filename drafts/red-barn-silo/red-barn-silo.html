How to draw
a red barn
and silo

Start with blocky building shapes, then add the roof, doors, fence, lane, and soft farm-field texture.

30 minMedium8 steps
Start drawing

From the archive

Let's draw a red barn and silo

Work lightly through the construction, then darken only the lines that help the finished sketch.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Graphite pencilWhatever you already have
  • Drawing paperWhatever you already have
  • EraserWhatever you already have
  • Optional red, green, and brown pencilsCompletely optional

No colored pencil? Graphite alone makes a complete sketch.

  1. 01

    Place the big blocks

    Draw a tall silo block on the left, a wide barn block on the right, and two light rolling field lines behind them.

    Sketch tip: Keep the barn and silo on the same ground line so the buildings feel planted.

  2. 02

    Build the roof shapes

    Round the silo top, add the barn's front gable, then pull a slanted side roof back to the right.

    Sketch tip: Put the roof peak over the front barn block before drawing the side roof.

  3. 03

    Add doors and windows

    Place a big double barn door, cross its panels with an X, then add a small upper window and a side shed door.

    Sketch tip: Use rectangles first. The X braces only work if the door frame is already square.

  4. 04

    Draw siding and roof lines

    Add vertical siding to the barn, light grid lines to the silo, and darker edges along the roof.

    Sketch tip: Keep these texture lines lighter than the outside contours so the forms stay readable.

  5. 05

    Set the fence and lane

    March fence posts from the foreground toward the barn, connect them with rails, then pull two lane edges from the door.

    Sketch tip: Make the nearest fence posts taller and darker. The smaller posts create depth.

  6. 06

    Add shrubs and road texture

    Draw low shrub clumps around the buildings, then add short broken strokes and pebble marks through the lane.

    Sketch tip: Let the lane marks get smaller as they approach the barn door.

  7. 07

    Shade the main forms

    Deepen the barn door, roof edge, silo side, and foreground fence, then add a few field strokes behind the buildings.

    Sketch tip: The darkest values belong in the door, under roof edges, and on the nearest fence posts.

  8. 08

    Finish with farm color

    Add loose red pencil to the barn, green to the shrubs and fields, and warm brown strokes through the dirt lane.

    Sketch tip: Do not color every space. Leaving paper gaps keeps the farm sketch light and handmade.
